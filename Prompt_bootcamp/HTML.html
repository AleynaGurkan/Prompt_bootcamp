Art Movements
Impressionism

Focused on capturing the fleeting effects of light and color, Impressionism offers a fresh, spontaneous look at everyday subjects.

Expressionism

Prioritizes the expression of emotional experience over physical reality, often using bold colors and dynamic compositions.

Cubism

Breaks objects into abstract, geometric forms, suggesting multiple viewpoints simultaneously.

Futurism

Emphasizes speed, technology, and dynamic movement, celebrating the energy of the modern world.

Surrealism

Explores the world of dreams and the subconscious, combining surprising, often bizarre imagery.

Abstract Expressionism

Focuses on the act of painting itself, using large, sweeping gestures to express personal freedom and emotional intensity.

Minimalism

Reduces art to its simplest, most essential forms and colors, striving for purity and clarity.

Pop Art

Draws from popular culture and mass media, presenting everyday images in bright, bold ways.

Op Art

Utilizes optical illusions and geometric patterns to create the impression of movement or vibrating spaces.

Dada

Rejects conventional art forms and logic, favoring nonsense and irrationality as a protest against the status quo.

Art Nouveau

Characterized by elegant, flowing lines and natural forms, aiming for a total art style that blends various elements harmoniously.

Neo-Impressionism

Advances Impressionism's interests in light and color through scientific methods and structured brushwork.
